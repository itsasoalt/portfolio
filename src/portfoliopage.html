All I know now about
My Portfolio

My Portfolio summarizes my very favorite projects and points out which mistakes I made and what I learnt. Because this is the design process for me: doing my best and trying to improve every time.

There are so many things I’ve done wrong yet, that I’ve learnt much more than if I’d had them wright the first time.

I introduce you to
Sokaire

Designing and HTML/CSS coding SOKAIRE’s webpage was my first experience freelancing in partnership with a fellow designer and friend Janire Dosantos. We wanted to offer all we were able to do, and so we did.

I think of this experience as the most valuable I’ve had up to now, because it was my first job as a wed designer and I did all the mistakes I had been warned not to do, I guess that I didn’t want to let any for the future.

I need it by the weekend. Actually, don’t worry, by next month is all right too.
  • I didn’t meet the client. I made a personal note not to allow this again. But I sincerely trusted my design partner Janire and I thought I had no need to meet the client.
  • We worked as the very next day was the deadline, but we lacked an awful lot of information and real content. The client wanted everything ASAP, but they wouldn’t handle us the content we required on time.
  • We didn’t prototype. Because of this we did a lot of waste work. HTML/CSS programming that we has to undo, redo, change… If I had known all I know now I would have spent a third of my time prototyping, a third waiting for the real content and a third actually coding.
  • We sticked to what we already knew, we didn’t try something new when designing. This led us to a crazy spaghetti HTML/CSS coding and not to learn any further coding resources.

I introduce you to my
Portfolio 2014

I stated myself as a designer with this personal portfolio design: go BIG or go home.

This was my final project of my introduction course to HTML/CSS and I wanted to use all the features I’ve learnt in class.

I didn’t pay too much attention to the design or content, I actually wanted it to be very visual and I wanted it to offer a experience to the viewer.

As I wanted to go big on my design, I went BIG on my mistakes too.

I wanted my portfolio to be responsive and cool that when performing it was almost frozen.
  • As I’ve said, I didn’t pay enough attention to the content. My photos were very high quality and damaged the performance of the full web.
  • There were some things I didn’t know how to do, and I did them anyway. When the responsive design changed the layout and placed the items en different ways I just sticked to the “do not show”, but it didn’t mean that they would “not charge”. Again, I was unconsciously damaging my personal portfolio website’s performance.
  • I don’t consider myself a graphic designer and I don’t particularly enjoy it, this time I had to do my own identity and couldn’t be less coherent. I created every item thinking of how I wanted to style them and how they would look good on the web, but I didn’t think of the brand itself.

I introduce you to
Naow

I have always tried to do my best in class, but probably during the NAOW workshop I learnt a hundred times more. If I had to think of a “worst case scenario” project, I would immediately think of NAOW. But yet, one of the coolest experiences I’ve had.

Worst case scenario: Friday, 2h to leave, half of the group missing and who remained weren’t willing to work. So we role-played a Web Design Studio: the teacher played our client, students acted as graphic designers, content creators and programmers, I was the project manager.

The assignment was to design a web-app to find and share cool activities to do in Bilbao, some kind of social network.

Well, I was in charge. And for everything I did right I made a mistake.

If you have finished help someone else. If you are done shut up or leave, but respect who is working.
  • I used the whiteboard to organize the steps, to list the folder system, to take down important notes so everyone could see them. Surprisingly, nobody was used to work as a team and they just focused on their task and never checked the whiteboard.
  • I set people in pairs to work so they could discuss ideas and feel supported when working. This was a good idea that led to a bad result: they sat on pairs with a single computer and discuss endlessly.
  • As I believe that communication is key and I wanted to ensure it, I checked everyone, reminded the color or font in use, helped them make a decision… I was really excited, but I wasn’t able to share my positive feelings, my team and I weren’t on the same page.
  • We didn’t finish the assignment on time. But it’s all right, because I trusted my team and our abilities. I take that with me for the future.
